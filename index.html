About Our Research

This research is a collaborative science project of the students of the Chinmaya International Residential School's IB Diplomma Programme. We aim to develop an interest towards the rich tapestry of the flora around us. We have conducted long researches about these plants and we now present it to you. This website is for anyone who wishes to learn more about the plants in our campus.

Our research focuses on identifying, documenting, and analyzing the medicinal properties of various plant species.

Featured Medicinal Plants

Red Ginger

Known for its striking red flower bracts, Red Ginger has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It's used in traditional medicine to aid digestion and reduce inflammation.

Peace Lily

Beyond its air-purifying abilities, Peace Lily contains compounds that can reduce airborne microbes. It's known to remove toxins like benzene, formaldehyde, and carbon monoxide from indoor environments.

Spider Plant

A resilient air purifier that removes harmful chemicals like formaldehyde and xylene. Spider plants are also known to reduce stress and improve mental well-being when kept in living spaces.

Chinese Banyan

Known for its impressive aerial roots, the Chinese Banyan has anti-inflammatory properties. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and reduce fever.

Zebra Plant

With its striking striped foliage, the Zebra Plant is known for its air-purifying qualities. It helps filter indoor air pollutants and is believed to have a calming effect on its surroundings.

Wamin Bamboo

This compact bamboo variety has anti-inflammatory properties. Its shoots are rich in nutrients and fiber, while extracts from its leaves are used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory conditions.

Ming Arabia

This elegant plant has adaptogenic properties that help reduce stress. Its extracts are used in traditional medicine to improve circulation and support respiratory health.

Bird of Paradise

With its striking flowers resembling exotic birds, this plant contains compounds with anti-inflammatory properties. In traditional medicine, its flower extracts are used to treat various skin conditions.

Ashoka

Revered in Ayurvedic medicine, the Ashoka tree has powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Its bark is traditionally used to treat gynecological disorders and has shown effectiveness in managing pain.

Asparagus Fern

Despite its name, the Asparagus Fern is not a true fern but has medicinal properties. Its roots contain steroidal saponins that have anti-inflammatory effects, used to treat urinary tract infections.

Ficus Microcarpa

This ornamental tree has compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its latex is used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and wounds.
